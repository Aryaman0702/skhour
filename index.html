Levels & Progression

OUR PROGRAM OVERVIEW

A structured progression path for skaters of all ages and backgrounds. Our levels ensure you build a solid foundation before advancing to technical mastery.

Group Level Focus Description
Red (Beginners)
 Snowplow Sam Balance on ice, basic forward & backward glides, safe falling & getting up, first steps & stride. Ideal for absolute beginners & young children.
Yellow (Gliders)
 Early Gliding Refining forward glides, introduction to forward edges, basic turns, simple stops (snowplow, T-stop), building confidence.
Green (Intermediate)
 Skill Refinement Backward skating, crossovers, intermediate turns, edge control (inside & outside edges), speed & endurance.
Blue (Advanced)
 Technical Growth Spins, intermediate jumps, glide transitions, choreography basics, speed & agility drills, complex footwork.
Purple (Masters)
 Figure & Hockey Power Skating Advanced jumps (axel, lutz, toe loop), intricate spins, figure skating techniques, hockey power skating drills, transitions, competitive routines.
Orange (Teens & Adults)
 Adult & Teen Track Age-appropriate balance, glides, steps, turns, endurance, recreational skating skills.

Got Questions?

WE HAVE ANSWERS

How do I choose the right skate?

+

Ensure the skate fits perfectly — not too big or small. Toes should rest comfortably without overlapping. Check for ankle support to prevent twisting. Your foot shouldn't move inside the skate. A snug fit is crucial for balance and control. Visit our Skating Guide for full details!

What's the appropriate age to start?

+

Any age 3 years and above is a great time to start. Even adults in their 40s or 50s are welcome. It's never too late to enjoy the fun of skating!

How to choose the right skate?

+

Ensure the skate fits perfectly—not too big or too small.

The toes should rest comfortably without overlapping, crumbling, or being squished.

Check for ankle support—the skate should provide enough support to keep your ankle stable and prevent it from twisting.

The skate shouldn’t be so big that your foot moves around inside. A snug fit is crucial for proper support and balance. Check out our Skating Guide for more info.

What’s the appropriate age to start?

+

Any age 3 years and above is a good time to start learning to skate.

Even adults in their 40s or 50s are welcome to join with the right guidance and support. It’s never too late to learn and enjoy the fun of skating!

How do I register?

+

You can contact us on WhatsApp or give us a call to register directly.

Alternatively, you can book online through our website by selecting your preferred location and timing for classes.

What should I expect in the first lesson?

+

Take it slow—getting on the ice for the first time can feel overwhelming for many.

Once you understand how to balance, everything will become a piece of cake.

The lesson will start with some floor exercises to help you get comfortable and understand basic movements before stepping on the rink.

What to prepare before the first lesson?

+

Arrive 15-20 minutes before class as tying skates takes time.

Bring woolen gloves for warmth and safety.

Wear comfortable clothes that allow easy movement.

For roller skating, wear a CSA approved caged helmet for safety.

For roller skating, bring a bike riding helmet and knee pads for additional protection.
